# Product Hunt Launch Kit

> Product Hunt skews more designer + maker than HN. The audience cares about polish, story, and category framing. Plan a separate launch (or +1 day after HN) — submitting to both same-day cannibalizes attention.

## When to launch

- **Day**: Tuesday or Wednesday
- **Time**: 12:01 AM **Pacific Time** (your day starts at 9:01 AM CET — be online for the first 4 hours)
- **Avoid**: launches stacked behind a Linear/Vercel/big-company launch on the same day
- **Hunter**: launch yourself unless you have a personal connection to a known hunter; impersonal big-hunter intros are net-neutral now

## Tagline (max 60 chars)

Candidates, scored on clarity and curiosity:

1. **`The voice keyboard that never leaves your laptop.`** (51 c) — recommended
2. **`Whisper-grade dictation, fully offline. $49 once.`** (49 c)
3. **`Voice typing without the subscription.`** (38 c)
4. **`Hold a hotkey, talk, paste anywhere. 100% local.`** (49 c)

Recommend #1 — leads with the privacy promise, "voice keyboard" reframes the category from "dictation tool" (boring) to "keyboard" (everyone has one).

## Description (max 260 chars)

```
Dictivo turns speech into polished text on your laptop — fully offline.
Hold a hotkey, talk, release, and the text appears wherever your cursor
is. No subscription, no cloud round-trip, no account. $49 once with 12
months of updates included.
```

253 chars. Hits: capability, mechanic, anti-pattern (no subscription),
price.

## First comment (the maker's introduction)

PH expects a first comment from the maker. Aim for ~150 words:

```
Hi Hunters — solo maker here. Dictivo is the voice dictation app I wanted
my partner (a writer, not a coder) to be able to install in 60 seconds.

It runs whisper.cpp locally on macOS: hold a hotkey, talk, release. The
polished transcript pastes into whatever app has focus — Notion, Slack,
the next email. Nothing about your audio ever leaves the laptop.

The pricing is the contrarian bit. $49 once, 12 months of updates
included. After that the version you have keeps working — you can
optionally renew for $24 to keep getting new versions and new
transcription models. Buy-once is the model I wanted for a tool I use
daily; "perpetual fallback" is in the EULA in writing.

Free tier with the `tiny` model if you want to try first.

What would you like dictated next? :)
```

## Gallery images (1270 × 760 px)

Required pieces, in order:

1. **Hero shot** — Dictivo's main window over a clean desktop. Show real transcribed text. Background: muted wallpaper, no notification bar.
2. **Animation / GIF** — the demo GIF from `marketing/` Remotion project. Critical that this is in slot 2 (PH auto-plays the second image).
3. **Settings → Local Engine** — show the hardware-aware tier card. Reinforces "smart" + "private."
4. **Companion floating window** — the small floating widget mid-dictation. Shows the actual UX, not just static screens.
5. **Pricing page screenshot** — your dictivo.app pricing card with the $49 / $24 split. Functions as a soft CTA.
6. **Comparison table** — Dictivo vs Wispr Flow vs Superwhisper vs MacWhisper (privacy / price / subscription / models). Convinces the comparison shopper.

Each image needs a 2x version (2540 × 1520) for retina. PH compresses heavily; export at high quality.

## Topics / categories

Tag with: `Mac` (essential), `Productivity` (essential), `Privacy`,
`Artificial Intelligence`, `Writing tools`.

Skip: `SaaS` (misleading — we're not), `Open Source` (the client isn't),
generic `AI` (over-tagged, signals desperation).

## Pricing card

PH shows a pricing block beside the description. Fill in:

- **One-time purchase**: $49
- **Renewal** (optional): $24 / year
- **Trial**: Free tier (no time limit)

## Maker comment cadence

- First 4 hours: respond within 5 minutes of every comment
- Hours 4–12: respond within 30 minutes
- Hours 12–24: respond within 2 hours
- Day 2+: respond within a working day

Pattern for replies: ack → real content → ask back. "Thanks! The reason
we [X] is [Y]. Would [Z use case] work for you?"

## What to ship to maintainers / press in the 48h before

Optional but cheap:

- DM to **Sindre Sorhus** (maker of Aiko) on Twitter — make it gracious; he might retweet
- Email **Federico Viticci (MacStories)** — only if you have a working demo and a clean DMG
- Post in **r/macapps** (your own thread, not a PH-link drop) — they hate cross-posts but love direct stories
- Submit to **Hacker Newsletter** the day-of (it's curated, no harm trying)

## Don't

- Don't ask friends to upvote on day-of. PH detects and penalizes hard.
- Don't post the PH link to /r/macapps or /r/macOSBeta. Both communities punish drive-by promo.
- Don't promise features in maker comments unless you can ship them in two weeks. Comments become permanent expectations.
- Don't reply to negative comments defensively. "Fair point, here's what I'm doing about it" beats every single argument.

## Day-after recap

Capture for your records:

- Total upvotes
- Position on the leaderboard at end of day
- Number of paying customers attributable (Lemon Squeezy referrer
  analytics filter `producthunt.com`)
- Top 3 surprising questions / objections — feed back into your FAQ

Whatever numbers come out, the PH launch is mostly a brand validation
event, not a primary distribution channel. The conversion rate is
usually 10x lower than HN. Use it for credibility, not survival.
